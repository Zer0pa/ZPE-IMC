We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in
ZPE-IMC a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of
age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex
characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of
experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal
appearance, race, caste, colour, religion, or sexual identity and
orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open,
welcoming, and evidence-led community.

---

<p>
  <img src=".github/assets/readme/section-bars/our-standards.svg" alt="OUR STANDARDS" width="100%">
</p>
### Expected behaviour

- Demonstrating empathy and respect toward other contributors
- Being respectful of differing technical opinions and
  interpretations — including inconclusive or negative results
- Giving and gracefully accepting evidence-backed feedback
- Accepting responsibility for mistakes and learning from them
- Focusing on what is best for the integrity of the system and
  the community
- Retaining and documenting negative results rather than
  suppressing them — this is a core scientific and community norm
  here, not optional

### Unacceptable behaviour

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments — including
  disparagement of contributors for negative findings
- Publishing others' private information without explicit
  permission
- Inflating technical claims beyond their evidenced scope —
  asserting human-equivalence, clinical efficacy, or regulatory
  fitness where the artifact trail does not support it
- Submitting contributions designed to launder IP, obscure
  prior art, or circumvent the Protected Architecture definition
  in SAL v6.0
- Sustained bad-faith engagement — repeatedly disputing
  evidence-backed findings without providing counter-evidence
- Any conduct that a reasonable person would consider
  inappropriate in a professional setting

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces — GitHub

comments, and any space where the project is officially

representing the project in public spaces, including use of an
official email address or acting as an appointed representative
at an event.

Instances of unacceptable behaviour may be reported to:

**`architects@zer0pa.ai`**

All reports will be reviewed promptly and investigated with
confidentiality. The reporter's identity will not be disclosed
without their explicit consent.

If a report concerns a maintainer, it may be escalated directly
to `architects@zer0pa.ai` for independent review.

<p>
  <img src=".github/assets/readme/section-bars/enforcement-guidelines.svg" alt="ENFORCEMENT GUIDELINES" width="100%">
</p>
Maintainers will follow these guidelines when determining
consequences for violations:

### 1. Correction

**Impact:** Minor inappropriate language or unprofessional
behaviour.
**Consequence:** Private written warning with explanation.
A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Impact:** A single incident or a pattern of minor violations.
**Consequence:** Formal warning with defined consequences for
continued behaviour. No unsolicited interaction with those
involved for a specified period. This includes community spaces
and external channels.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Impact:** A serious violation or sustained inappropriate
behaviour.
**Consequence:** Temporary ban from all community interaction
and contribution. Violating the terms of a temporary ban may
result in a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Impact:** Demonstrated pattern of violations, harassment,
sustained bad-faith technical conduct, or IP circumvention
attempts.
**Consequence:** Permanent ban from all public community
interaction and contribution. Contributions already merged
may be reviewed and reverted at maintainer discretion.

<p>
  <img src=".github/assets/readme/section-bars/evidence-discipline-as-community-norm.svg" alt="EVIDENCE DISCIPLINE AS COMMUNITY NORM" width="100%">
</p>
ZPE-IMC operates under falsifiability-first principles. Within
this community that means:

- Claims require evidence. Asserting that something works without
  an artifact trail is not a contribution — it is noise.
- Negative results are protected. A contributor who surfaces a
  genuine failure is performing a service, not an attack.
- Scope discipline is non-negotiable. Claims must not be inflated
  beyond what the evidence supports. This applies to contributors,
  maintainers, and the project itself.
- Explicit UNVERIFIED example: broad statements such as
  "no direct regression evidence across all lanes" stay UNVERIFIED
  until a single consolidated artifact is provided for that exact
  sentence.

Violations of evidence discipline that affect the integrity of
the proof corpus — not merely style disagreements — will be
treated under the enforcement guidelines above.
